Welcome

The Israel Deep Sea explorers is a group of technical divers committed to locating, diving and documenting shipwrecks and geological sites in the Mediterranean Sea off the coast of Israel.

We have worked together since 2005, bringing new and old wrecks, canyons and reefs to public knowledge. Much of our information has come through our collaboration with Roni Sade and his groundbreaking research utilizing multi-beam sonar technology and data processing - thanks Roni :-) Other leads come from old fishermen, boat captains and fellow divers. Our group currently numbers 14 people (see the bios section). Prospective members can apply through the site.

The Israeli Mediterranean coast is not famous for its diving. In contrast, our little strip of desert on the coast of the Red Sea is well known throughout the world for warm water reef diving. However, we in the IDx believe the Mediterranean with its three thousand years of maritime history, temperate climate (18c - 31c) and easy access, is one of the best diving sites in the world; with possibly hundreds of wrecks dating from Physician times and Roman era amphorae, through WWII war casualties, all the way to modern barges and trawling wrecks, there is still much yet to be explored here.
